# Pattern Family 11 — Starting a Conversation Naturally

## Hindi Pattern

- और बताओ।
- और सुनाओ।
- क्या चल रहा है?
- क्या नया?
- और क्या हाल?

---

## Natural English Expressions

| Expression | Register | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| What's been going on? | Casual | Great after not talking for a while. |
| What's new? | Casual | Asks for recent updates. |
| How have things been? | Neutral | Friendly and natural. |
| What's been keeping you busy? | Neutral | Focuses on recent activities. |
| So, what's going on? | Casual | Good conversation opener. |

---

## Nuance

These expressions invite conversation rather than asking for a detailed report.

---

## Examples

### 👥 Friends

**Hindi:** और बताओ, क्या चल रहा है?

**Natural English:** So, what's been going on?

### 💼 Office

**Hindi:** और, प्रोजेक्ट कैसा चल रहा है?

**Natural English:** So, how have things been with the project?

### ☕ Café

**Hindi:** क्या नया?

**Natural English:** What's new?

### 📱 Phone

**Hindi:** और सुनाओ, सब ठीक?

**Natural English:** What's been going on? Everything good?

### 🏠 Family

**Hindi:** और क्या हाल?

**Natural English:** How have things been?

---

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Mistake

Tell me more.

### ✅ Natural English

What's been going on?

### Why?

"Tell me more" is used after someone has already started speaking, not as a greeting.

---

## Similar English Expressions

| Expression | Difference |
|---|---|
| How's everything? | General well-being. |
| Anything new? | Recent updates. |
| How's life? | Broader and informal. |

---

## Memory Tip

> Think of these as conversation openers.

## Quick Recap

**Most common:** What's new?

**Best opener:** What's been going on?

**Avoid:** Tell me more.
